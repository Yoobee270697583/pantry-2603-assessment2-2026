This is the Cooked Meals screen. Content coming soon.

### "Cooked Meals" - Meals marked as cooked move from Planned Meals into a timestamped log here - Records: recipe, date cooked - Pantry inventory auto-deducts ingredients used in the cooked recipe (deduction happens at the moment "Mark as cooked" is tapped in Planned Meals) - Builds a personal eating history to inform future meal planning - **Per-meal card** — tapping/clicking the meal card always opens the full recipe spec - **Buttons per meal:** - **Cook again** — adds another instance of the meal back into Planned Meals - **Undo** — for an accidental/mistaken "mark as cooked": removes the cooked-meal entry **and restores the deducted ingredients to the pantry**, effectively reversing the cook (the meal can optionally return to Planned Meals). This is the true "I didn't actually cook this" path - **Delete** — for genuinely-cooked meals the user just wants to remove from their history: removes the log entry **only**, with no effect on pantry inventory - Splitting Undo and Delete separates the two distinct intents cleanly (reverse a mistake vs. tidy history), so neither needs to guess the user's meaning - **Button layout/hierarchy:** **Cook again** is the primary, always-visible action on the card. **Undo** and **Delete** are tucked into an overflow menu / the expanded card view rather than sitting as exposed buttons — keeps each card uncluttered (important on mobile) and keeps the destructive/reversal actions one deliberate step away - **Design consideration:** Delete may still warrant a light confirm step since it's destructive to history, but it no longer has to carry the inventory-restoration ambiguity — that's Undo's job {% endblock %}
